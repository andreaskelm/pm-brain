# Credits & Attributions

This repository builds on frameworks and methods from product management thought leaders. Where frameworks are directly based on or inspired by specific methodologies, they are credited below.

**Using this repo:** For where to start (product thinking, braindump-first workflow, and navigation to Strategy, Discovery, Execution, Communication), see [0-start-here-product-thinking.md](02-Methods-and-Tools/2.0-Foundations/2.0.1-Mental-Models/6-Product-Sense-Development/0-start-here-product-thinking.md) and [PRODUCT-SENSE-RULES.md](PRODUCT-SENSE-RULES.md). For setup and usage guidelines, see [SETUP.md](SETUP.md) and [GUIDELINES.md](GUIDELINES.md).

## Frameworks & Methods

### Continuous Discovery Habits
- **Source:** Teresa Torres, [Continuous Discovery Habits](https://www.producttalk.org/continuous-discovery-habits/)
- **Book:** [Continuous Discovery Habits](https://www.amazon.com/Continuous-Discovery-Habits-Discover-Products/dp/1736633309)
- **Support:** [Product Talk](https://www.producttalk.org/)

### Jobs-to-be-Done
- **Source:** Clayton Christensen, Tony Ulwick, Bob Moesta
- **Book:** [Jobs to be Done: Theory to Practice](https://jobstobedone.org/)
- **Support:** [JTBD Framework](https://jobstobedone.org/)

### OKRs
- **Source:** John Doerr, [Measure What Matters](https://www.whatmatters.com/)
- **Book:** [Measure What Matters](https://www.amazon.com/Measure-What-Matters-Google-Foundation/dp/0525536221)
- **Support:** [What Matters](https://www.whatmatters.com/)

### North Star Framework
- **Source:** Amplitude (Sean Ellis, John Cutler, and team)
- **Resource:** [North Star Playbook](https://amplitude.com/north-star)
- **Support:** [Amplitude Blog](https://amplitude.com/blog/north-star-metric)

### RICE Scoring
- **Source:** Sean McBride, Intercom
- **Resource:** [Intercom Blog - RICE](https://www.intercom.com/blog/rice-simple-prioritization-for-product-managers/)
- **Support:** [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com/)

### ICE Scoring
- **Source:** Intercom (related to RICE framework)
- **Resource:** [Intercom Blog](https://www.intercom.com/blog/)
- **Note:** ICE (Impact, Confidence, Ease) is a simplified variant of RICE

### Double Diamond
- **Source:** Design Council (UK)
- **Resource:** [Design Council - Double Diamond](https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/our-work/skills-learning/resources/11-ways-get-started-design-methods/)
- **Support:** [Design Council](https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/)

### Opportunity Solution Tree
- **Source:** Teresa Torres (part of Continuous Discovery Habits)
- **Resource:** [Product Talk - Opportunity Solution Tree](https://www.producttalk.org/opportunity-solution-tree/)
- **Note:** Included in Continuous Discovery Habits methodology (credited above)

### HEART Framework (Personas)
- **Source:** Google (Kerry Rodden, Hilary Hutchinson, Xin Fu)
- **Resource:** [Google Research - HEART Framework](https://research.google/pubs/pub36299/)
- **Paper:** "Measuring the User Experience on a Large Scale: User-Centered Metrics for Web Applications"

### 4Cs Model (Brain-Based Learning)
- **Source:** Based on brain-based instructional design principles
- **Note:** Combines principles from cognitive science and instructional design
- **Related:** Works by David Sousa, Eric Jensen on brain-based learning

### MoSCoW Prioritization
- **Source:** DSDM (Dynamic Systems Development Method)
- **Resource:** [DSDM Consortium](https://www.agilebusiness.org/)
- **Note:** MoSCoW (Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have) is a prioritization technique from the DSDM agile framework

### Kano Model
- **Source:** Noriaki Kano (1980s)
- **Resource:** [Kano Model - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kano_model)
- **Paper:** "Attractive Quality and Must-Be Quality" (1984)
- **Note:** Revolutionary framework showing customer satisfaction is emotional, not linear

### AARRR Pirate Metrics
- **Source:** Dave McClure, 500 Startups
- **Resource:** [Startup Metrics for Pirates](https://500hats.typepad.com/500blogs/2007/06/startup-metrics.html)
- **Support:** [500 Startups](https://500.co/)
- **Note:** Growth framework measuring Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral

### Product Judgment Test (Weighted Brier Score)
- **Source:** Viveck P Kumar (product application); Brier score from Glenn W. Brier (1950), standard in forecasting
- **Resource:** [The Product Judgment Test](https://www.notion.so/The-Product-Judgment-Test-2e92182754b68081a535fd7f507f7b91) (Notion)
- **Note:** Forecast-before-ship calibration tracker using a **Weighted Brier Score**. Viveck P Kumar adapted the Brier score for product work (bet type, novelty weights, outcome mapping). Used in `00-Meta/0.3-Product-Judgment-Test/`.

### Shreyas Doshi – PM Senses & Work Levels
- **Source:** Shreyas Doshi (ex-Stripe, ex-Google, ex-Twitter)
- **Resource:** [Shreyas Doshi](https://twitter.com/shreyas) (talks, threads, Reforge)
- **Note:** **Three Essential PM Senses** (Product / Execution / Analytical) referenced in self-assessment template (`02-Methods-and-Tools/2.0-Foundations/2.0.1-Mental-Models/6-Product-Sense-Development/templates/2-self-assessment-template.md`). **Three Levels of Work** (Impact / Execution / Optics) referenced in Work Levels mental model.

### Playing to Win (Strategic Choice Cascade)
- **Source:** A.G. Lafley (ex-P&G CEO) & Roger Martin (Rotman School)
- **Book:** [Playing to Win: How Strategy Really Works](https://www.amazon.com/Playing-Win-Strategy-Really-Works/dp/142218739X) (2013, Harvard Business Review Press)
- **Note:** Five-question Strategic Choice Cascade for markets, positioning, and capabilities. Used in `02-Methods-and-Tools/2.1-Strategy/2.1.1-Strategic-Foundations/3-Playing-To-Win/`.

### Good Strategy/Bad Strategy (The Kernel)
- **Source:** Richard Rumelt (UCLA Anderson School of Management)
- **Book:** [Good Strategy Bad Strategy](https://www.amazon.com/Good-Strategy-Bad-Difference-Matters/dp/0307886239) (2011)
- **Note:** Strategy kernel (diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent action) for evaluating and teaching good strategy. Used in `02-Methods-and-Tools/2.1-Strategy/2.1.1-Strategic-Foundations/2-Good-Strategy-Bad-Strategy/`.

### Strategy Blocks
- **Source:** Chandra Janakiraman (via Lenny's Newsletter)
- **Resource:** [Strategy Blocks – An Operator's Guide](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/strategy-blocks-an-operators-guide)
- **Note:** Operational process for creating strategy. Used in `02-Methods-and-Tools/2.1-Strategy/2.1.1-Strategic-Foundations/1-Strategy-Blocks/`. Often combined with Playing to Win (choices) and Good Strategy Kernel (validation).

### Tactical / Operational / Strategic Work Levels
- **Source:** Melissa Perri, [Escaping the Build Trap](https://www.producttalk.org/2018/08/escaping-the-build-trap/)
- **Book:** [Escaping the Build Trap](https://www.amazon.com/Escaping-Build-Trap-Product-Management/dp/149197379X)
- **Note:** Three levels of PM work (Tactical, Operational, Strategic) by time horizon and impact. Used in `02-Methods-and-Tools/2.0-Foundations/2.0.1-Mental-Models/3-Work-Levels/`.
