## Key Principles Summary

### The 4Cs Structure:
- **C1 - Connections** (10-20%): Link to existing knowledge and experience
- **C2 - Concepts** (25-30%): Multi-sensory introduction of new information  
- **C3 - Concrete Practice** (40-50%): Hands-on application and skill building
- **C4 - Conclusions** (10-15%): Synthesis, reflection, and commitment to action

### Brain-Based Design Elements:
- **Movement** trumps sitting
- **Writing** trumps reading  
- **Talking** trumps listening
- **Shorter** trumps longer
- **Images** trump words
- **Different** trumps same

## When to Use This Framework

### Ideal For:
- Professional development courses
- Skills-based training programs
- Workshop design
- Team learning sessions
- Change management training

### Best Fit Duration:
- Single sessions: 2-4 hours
- Multi-session courses: 2-8 sessions
- Workshop series: Half-day to multi-day formats

### Target Audience:
- Adult learners in professional contexts
- Groups of 8-25 participants
- Mixed experience levels within same domain

## Success Metrics

### During Course Indicators:
- High engagement in activities (>80% active participation)
- Quality peer teaching moments
- Relevant questions and examples from participants
- Time spent in practice vs. concept phases (40-50% practice)

### Post-Course Indicators:
- Participants applying frameworks in real work (within 2 weeks)
- Requests for follow-up sessions or advanced topics
- Behavior change observations from managers/peers
- Measurable improvements in target competencies

## Customization Notes

### For Different Industries:
- Replace examples with industry-specific scenarios
- Use domain-relevant terminology and frameworks
- Include industry stakeholder perspectives

### For Different Experience Levels:
- **Beginners**: More connection time, simpler concepts, guided practice
- **Advanced**: Less connection, complex scenarios, peer teaching opportunities
- **Mixed Groups**: Tiered activities, flexible grouping strategies

### For Different Formats:
- **Virtual**: Add chat interactions, breakout rooms, shared digital tools
- **Hybrid**: Balance in-person kinesthetic with digital collaboration
- **Asynchronous**: Include reflection journals, peer feedback loops
